Eye Strain & Digital Fatigue

Staring at screens for prolonged periods causes digital eye strain, characterized by dry eyes, headaches, blurred vision, and neck/shoulder pain. The high-energy visible blue light emitted by screens contributes to these symptoms and may cause long-term retinal damage.

Prevention: Follow the 20-20-20 rule (every 20 minutes, look at something 20 feet away for 20 seconds), adjust screen brightness, and use blue light filters.

Practical Tips for Digital Balance

Implement these strategies to maintain healthy screen-time habits

Time Blocking

Schedule specific times for different screen activities and stick to them. Use a timer to enforce limits.

Blue Light Filters

Enable night mode on devices after sunset or use blue light filtering glasses to reduce sleep disruption.

Active Breaks

Take 5-minute movement breaks every hour. Stretch, walk, or do light exercises to counteract sedentary time.

Notification Management

Disable non-essential notifications and batch-check communications instead of constant monitoring.

Digital-Free Zones

Establish screen-free areas (bedroom, dining table) and times (first/last hour of the day).

Alternative Activities

Replace some screen time with offline hobbies: reading physical books, outdoor activities, or crafts.

7-Day Digital Balance Challenge

Complete these daily challenges to build healthier habits

Day 1: Screen-Free Morning

Spend the first hour after waking up without checking any screens.

Day 2: Digital Detox Meal

Day 3: Notification Cleanse

Day 4: Active Break Routine

Day 5: Blue Light Awareness
