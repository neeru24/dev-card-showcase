Case File #001

Difficulty: Beginner

The Locked Room Mystery

Unsolved

Detective Miller arrives at the mansion of the late Professor Aris. The professor was found dead in his study, which was locked from the inside. The only key was found on his desk. There are no hidden passages, and the windows are sealed shut.

Security footage shows that no one entered or left the study all day. The detective concludes that the professor must have died by suicide, despite no suicide note being found and the professor having no history of depression.

Clues:

  • The room was locked from the inside with no other exits
  • The only key was found inside the room on the desk
  • Security footage shows no one entered or left
  • No suicide note was found
  • The professor had booked a vacation for the following week

The Logical Flaw

What is the incorrect assumption or logical fallacy in Detective Miller's conclusion?

Consider whether the evidence necessarily leads to the conclusion, or if there are alternative explanations.

About Logical Fallacies

False Dilemma

Presenting only two options when more exist.

Hasty Generalization

Drawing a conclusion from insufficient evidence.

Circular Reasoning

Using the conclusion as evidence for itself.

Post Hoc Fallacy

Assuming that because B followed A, A caused B.
