Pedestrian Safety

Safe Crossing Practices

Follow these guidelines for safe road crossing:

  • Always use designated crosswalks or intersections
  • Look left, right, and left again before crossing
  • Make eye contact with drivers before proceeding
  • Continue looking while crossing
  • Follow pedestrian signals, not just traffic lights

Visibility & Awareness

Stay visible and alert at all times:

  • Wear bright/reflective clothing at night
  • Put away phones and avoid distractions
  • Use sidewalks whenever available
  • Walk facing traffic if no sidewalk exists
  • Watch for turning vehicles at intersections

Special Considerations

Extra precautions for vulnerable pedestrians:

  • Children should always cross with an adult
  • Elderly pedestrians need extra crossing time
  • Keep pets on short leashes near roads
  • Be extra cautious in poor weather conditions
  • Never assume a driver sees you

Cyclist Safety

Essential Gear

Proper equipment is critical for safety:

  • Always wear a properly fitted helmet
  • Use lights (white front, red rear) at night
  • Wear bright, reflective clothing
  • Ensure your bike has reflectors
  • Regularly check brakes and tires

Hand Signals & Communication

Communicate your intentions clearly:

  • Left turn: extend left arm straight out
  • Right turn: extend right arm straight out OR left arm up at 90°
  • Stopping: extend left arm down at 90° with palm facing back
  • Make eye contact with drivers at intersections
  • Use bell or voice to alert pedestrians

Riding Practices

Follow these rules of the road:

  • Ride in the same direction as traffic
  • Obey all traffic signs and signals
  • Use bike lanes when available
  • Maintain a safe distance from parked cars
  • Watch for opening car doors

Common Mistakes to Avoid

DO's

  • Make eye contact with drivers before crossing
  • Use designated crosswalks and bike lanes
  • Wear bright/reflective clothing in low light
  • Stay alert and avoid distractions
  • Follow traffic signals and signs
  • Check for turning vehicles at intersections

DON'Ts

  • Assume drivers can see you
  • Cross between parked cars
  • Use phones while walking or cycling
  • Ride against traffic (wrong way)
  • Ignore hand signals and traffic rules
  • Wear dark clothing at night
