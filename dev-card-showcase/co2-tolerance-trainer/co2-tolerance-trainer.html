CO₂ Tolerance Training Guide

Safety First

  • Always practice in a safe environment with someone nearby
  • Stop immediately if you feel dizzy, lightheaded, or uncomfortable
  • Consult a healthcare professional before starting training
  • Start with short holds and gradually increase duration

Training Techniques

  • Voluntary Apnea: Hold breath after normal exhalation
  • Static Apnea: Hold breath in a relaxed position
  • Dynamic Apnea: Hold breath while performing light exercise
  • CO₂ Tables: Structured training with increasing hold times

Benefits

  • Improved respiratory efficiency
  • Better stress management and anxiety reduction
  • Enhanced endurance for athletes
  • Increased lung capacity and diaphragm control
