Understanding Joint Loading

Joint Loading Basics

Joint loading refers to the forces and stresses placed on joints during movement. Proper loading is essential for joint health, but excessive or imbalanced loading can lead to injury.

Key Joints to Monitor

  • Shoulders: Rotator cuff, glenohumeral joint
  • Elbows: Humeroulnar, radioulnar joints
  • Hips: Acetabulofemoral joint
  • Knees: Tibiofemoral, patellofemoral joints
  • Ankles: Talocrural joint

Loading Factors

  • Exercise selection and volume
  • Weight/resistance used
  • Repetition ranges
  • Training frequency
  • Recovery between sessions
