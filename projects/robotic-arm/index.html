Robotic Arm Kinematics Guide

ğŸ¯ What is this?

This is a procedural animation engine that uses FABRIK (Forward And Backward Reaching Inverse Kinematics). Instead of telling each joint exactly how to rotate (Forward Kinematics), you tell the tip of the arm where to go, and the computer works backward to instantly calculate the rotation of every single joint required to reach that target!

The canvas acts as a standard Cartesian plane to help you visualize the trigonometry happening in the background:

  • The Axes: The faint dashed lines crossing the screen represent the X-axis (horizontal) and Y-axis (vertical). The base of the robotic arm is anchored exactly at the origin (0,0) where they intersect.
  • Angle from X-Axis: This telemetry metric calculates the trajectory of the arm's tip relative to the horizontal line. 0Â° means the arm is pointing perfectly right (East).
  • Angle from Y-Axis: This metric calculates the trajectory relative to the vertical line. 0Â° means the arm is pointing perfectly up (North).
