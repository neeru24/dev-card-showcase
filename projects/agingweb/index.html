The Passage of Time

Welcome to a space that remembers. Unlike static web pages that remain frozen in perfection, this environment acknowledges the entropy of the universe. Every second since your first arrival is counted, calculated, and etched into the visual fabric of this document.

Digital Erosion

Bits do not rot. But we can simulate the wear. We introduce random bit-flips, color shifts, and coordinate displacements to mimic the physical weathering of materials. What you see now will not exist in this form within an hour.

Temporal Logic

The engine runs on a monotonic clock synchronized to your local machine. It defines epochs of stability and eras of chaos. Currently, we are observing the structural integrity of the DOM elements as they withstand the pressure of simulated time.

Visual Noise

Perlin noise and white noise generators create a dynamic film grain that accumulates on the viewport. This "digital dust" obscures content, forcing the user to strain, much like reading an ancient manuscript.

Manifesto of Decay

In the beginning, there was code, and the code was clean. But functionality is fleeting. We build systems to last, yet we abandon them. This project is a memento mori for the web. It asks: what does a website look like when it creates its own ruins?

Observe the margins. Watch the typography. The kerning will fail. The line-height will collapse. Colors will drift towards the gray. This is not a bug; it is the feature of existence.
