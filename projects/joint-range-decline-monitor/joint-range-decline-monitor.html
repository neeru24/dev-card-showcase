Joint Mobility Assessment Guide

How to Measure ROM

  • Use a goniometer or smartphone app for accurate measurements
  • Measure in a comfortable, warm environment
  • Have someone assist you for hard-to-reach joints
  • Take measurements at the same time of day for consistency
  • Record both active (self-movement) and passive (assisted) ROM

Normal Range of Motion

  • Shoulder: Flexion 180°, Extension 60°, Abduction 180°
  • Elbow: Flexion 160°, Extension 0° (full extension)
  • Hip: Flexion 140°, Extension 30°, Abduction 50°
  • Knee: Flexion 160°, Extension 0° (full extension)
  • Ankle: Dorsiflexion 20°, Plantarflexion 60°

Preventing Decline

  • Regular stretching and mobility exercises
  • Low-impact activities like swimming or walking
  • Strength training to support joint stability
  • Maintain healthy weight to reduce joint stress
  • Stay hydrated and eat anti-inflammatory foods
  • Consider physical therapy if decline is rapid
