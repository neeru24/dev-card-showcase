Common Causes of Exam Stress

  • Fear of failure or disappointing others
  • Lack of preparation or poor time management
  • High expectations from self, family, or teachers
  • Comparing yourself to classmates
  • Unfamiliar exam formats or content
  • Pressure to achieve specific grades for future goals

Symptoms of Anxiety

  • Physical: Headaches, nausea, rapid heartbeat, sweating
  • Emotional: Irritability, feeling overwhelmed, panic
  • Cognitive: Racing thoughts, blanking out, negative self-talk
  • Behavioral: Procrastination, avoidance, changes in sleep/eating
  • Remember: Some stress is normal, but excessive anxiety needs attention

Healthy Coping Techniques

  • Study Smart: Use spaced repetition, practice tests, and pomodoro technique
  • Mindfulness: 5-minute meditation before studying
  • Physical Health: Regular sleep, balanced nutrition, daily movement
  • Social Support: Form study groups, talk to mentors
  • Positive Self-Talk: Replace "I can't" with "I'm learning"
  • Break Tasks Down: Divide material into manageable chunks

What to Avoid During Exams

  • All-nighters: Sleep consolidates memory
  • Excessive caffeine: Increases anxiety and disrupts sleep
  • Social media comparison: Everyone shows highlight reels
  • Isolation: Balance study time with social connection
  • Catastrophizing: One exam doesn't define your future
  • Skipping meals: Brain needs fuel to function optimally
