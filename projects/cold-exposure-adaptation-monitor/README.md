2. **Log Your First Session**: Fill out the session form with your exposure details

1. **Select Exposure Type**: Choose from predefined options or select "Other"

3. **Temperature (Optional)**: Note the temperature if known
4. **Comfort Level**: Rate your comfort on a scale of 1-10
5. **Add Notes**: Include any observations or feelings about the session
6. **Submit**: Click "Log Session" to save your data

### Understanding Your Data
- **Adaptation Score**: Higher scores indicate better cold tolerance and adaptation
- **Weekly Sessions**: Aim for 3-5 sessions per week for optimal progress
- **Duration Trends**: Monitor whether your session lengths are increasing over time
- **Temperature Tolerance**: Track the lowest temperatures you can comfortably handle

## üõ°Ô∏è Safety Guidelines

### Important Warnings
- **Consult a Doctor**: Always consult with a healthcare professional before starting cold exposure therapy
- **Start Slowly**: Begin with short exposures (1-2 minutes) and gradually increase
- **Listen to Your Body**: Stop immediately if you experience pain, dizziness, or discomfort
- **Medical Conditions**: People with heart conditions, high blood pressure, or other health issues should avoid cold exposure

### Safe Practice Guidelines
1. **Warm Up First**: Ensure your body is warm before exposure
2. **Gradual Progression**: Increase duration/temperature by no more than 10-20% per week
3. **Hydration**: Stay well-hydrated before and after sessions
4. **Recovery**: Allow adequate recovery time between intense sessions
5. **Supervision**: Have someone nearby for longer or more intense exposures

### Emergency Signs
Stop immediately and seek medical attention if you experience:
- Chest pain or irregular heartbeat
- Severe dizziness or fainting
- Extreme shivering or inability to warm up
- Numbness or loss of sensation
- Confusion or disorientation

## üìä Adaptation Levels

### Beginner (0-24 points)
- Focus on consistency over intensity
- 2-3 sessions per week, 1-3 minutes each
- Room temperature water or slightly cool

### Intermediate (25-49 points)
- Building tolerance and confidence
- 3-4 sessions per week, 2-5 minutes each
- Cooler water temperatures

### Advanced (50-74 points)
- Significant adaptation achieved
- 4-5 sessions per week, 3-8 minutes each
- Cold water temperatures

### Elite (75-100 points)
- High level of cold tolerance
- 5+ sessions per week, 5+ minutes each
- Very cold temperatures or ice exposure
